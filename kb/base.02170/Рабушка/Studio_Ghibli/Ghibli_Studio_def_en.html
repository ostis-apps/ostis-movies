Studio Ghibli is a Japanese animation studio. It was founded in 1985 by director and screenwriter Hayao Miyazaki together with his colleague and friend Isao Takahata with the support of Tokuma, which later together with Walt Disney will distribute "Princess Mononoke" and "Spirited Away". The studio logo depicts Totoro, a character from the movie "My Neighbor Totoro", released Studio Ghibli in 1988. The soundtrack for most of the films was written by Jo Hisaishi. Studio Ghibli was one of the first Japanese companies to actively use computers in animation. The studio was founded in the city of Musashino.
