When the former Marine Clay got a job as a gardener in the villa of an elderly man with a quarrelsome character, he was surprised to learn that it was James Weil, a brilliant director and creator of the Frankenstein films that went down in the history of world cinema. Lonely and forgotten by everyone except Hannah's faithful servant, Whale paints and recalls the days of former glory. He opens up to Clay an exciting world of art and, fascinated by its masculine beauty, persuades him to pose for a portrait. Suddenly, these so different people come to the conclusion that they have a lot in common, and become friends, despite everything that separates them.
